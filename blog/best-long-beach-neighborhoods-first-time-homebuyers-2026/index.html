Best Long Beach Neighborhoods for First-Time Homebuyers in 2026

Long Beach can be a strong option for first-time buyers who want coastal access, city energy, and a variety of property types.

Published May 20, 2026 by Dianna Brang

Quick Take

First-time buyers should compare Long Beach neighborhoods by payment, property type, HOA dues, parking, commute, and future resale appeal instead of chasing one blanket idea of affordability.

Compared with many Orange County beach cities, Long Beach often gives buyers more ways to enter the market, especially through condos, townhomes, smaller single-family homes, and older homes that may need updates.

That said, 2026 buyers still need to be realistic. Long Beach prices remain high, mortgage rates continue to affect affordability, and move-in-ready homes in desirable neighborhoods can still sell quickly.

As of April 2026, Zillow reported the average Long Beach home value at $862,599, up 1.0% over the past year, with homes going pending in around 16 days. Redfin's March 2026 data showed a median sale price of $905,000 and an average of 45 days on market.

1. Bixby Knolls

Best for: buyers who want neighborhood charm, restaurants, and a more established feel.

Bixby Knolls is one of Long Beach's most recognizable neighborhoods. It has tree-lined streets, older homes, local businesses, and a strong neighborhood identity. It may not be the cheapest option, but it is a good fit for buyers who want a long-term home in a stable area.

  • Single-family homes
  • Character homes
  • Local restaurants and shops
  • A neighborhood feel without leaving Long Beach

2. Wrigley

Best for: buyers looking for relative value and central access.

Wrigley is often worth watching for first-time buyers because it can offer more approachable pricing than some coastal Long Beach neighborhoods. Homes vary widely, so buyers should pay attention to block-by-block differences, property condition, parking, and commute routes.

  • Starter homes
  • Buyers open to cosmetic updates
  • Central Long Beach access
  • Value-focused buyers

3. Downtown Long Beach

Best for: condo buyers, walkability, and urban lifestyle.

Downtown Long Beach is one of the most practical entry points for buyers who are open to condos. It gives access to restaurants, entertainment, the waterfront, transit, and high-rise or mid-rise condo options.

  • First-time condo buyers
  • Low-maintenance living
  • Walkability
  • Buyers who do not need a large yard

Things to watch: HOA dues, parking, building reserves, rental restrictions, and special assessments.

4. Bluff Heights

Best for: buyers who want charm and beach proximity.

Bluff Heights is attractive because of its historic homes, neighborhood character, and access to nearby beach areas. It can be more expensive than entry-level neighborhoods, but it may work for buyers with a larger budget or those considering smaller homes or condos nearby.

  • Craftsman-style homes
  • Beach-adjacent lifestyle
  • Walkability
  • Long-term resale appeal

5. Traffic Circle / East Long Beach

Best for: commuters and buyers who want practical access.

The Traffic Circle and East Long Beach areas can be useful for buyers who want access to freeways, shopping, Cal State Long Beach, and nearby Orange County. Property types vary, so this area is worth searching carefully.

  • Commuters
  • Condos and townhomes
  • Access to OC and LA
  • Buyers who want convenience

6. Lakewood Village

Best for: buyers who want a suburban feel within Long Beach.

Lakewood Village offers a more residential feel and is close to the Long Beach/Lakewood border. It can be attractive for buyers who want a single-family home environment but still want to remain in Long Beach.

  • Single-family homes
  • Quieter streets
  • Traditional neighborhood living
  • Access to nearby shopping and freeways

First-time buyer resources in 2026

Long Beach buyers should look into assistance programs early. The City of Long Beach points buyers toward federal, state, and local homebuyer assistance resources, including CalHFA programs.

CalHFA offers homebuyer loan programs for California buyers, and first-time buyers using CalHFA programs may need to complete homebuyer education. The California Dream For All program is designed for eligible first-generation homebuyers and can provide down payment or closing cost assistance through a shared appreciation loan structure. Program windows and eligibility can change, so buyers should confirm details directly with CalHFA and an approved lender.

Tips for first-time buyers in Long Beach

Before making offers, get clear on:

  • Your true monthly payment, not just purchase price
  • HOA dues if buying a condo
  • Property taxes and insurance
  • Parking
  • Inspection issues
  • Commute times
  • Flood zones or coastal considerations
  • Future resale appeal

The California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation recommends that first-time buyers understand costs beyond the mortgage, including property taxes, homeowners insurance, inspections, maintenance, closing costs, and HOA fees where applicable.

Bottom line

The best Long Beach neighborhood for a first-time buyer depends on budget and lifestyle. If you want walkability, look at Downtown Long Beach or Bluff Heights. If you want relative value, look at Wrigley or parts of East Long Beach. If you want a stronger neighborhood feel, consider Bixby Knolls or Lakewood Village.
